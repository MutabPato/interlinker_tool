Privacy Policy

Last updated: {{ now|date:"F j, Y" }}

This Privacy Policy explains how Interlinker (the "Service", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you visit and use interlinker. It also describes the choices you have regarding your data.

1. Key takeaways

  • We process the account details you provide (such as email, username, and password hashes) to authenticate you and secure your workspace.
  • We store the sitemap data you ingest, generated internal-link HTML, and run history so that you can revisit past results.
  • We do not sell your data. We only share it with trusted service providers or when required by law.
  • You can request deletion of your account and associated data at any time by contacting us.

2. Information we collect

Account and authentication data

When you create an account or sign in, we collect:

  • Identification: username, display name, and email address.
  • Credentials: salted and hashed password managed through Django's authentication framework.
  • Session data: cookies issued by Django to keep you signed in.

Operational data

  • Sitemaps and link inventories: URLs, slugs, and metadata that you import for the purpose of generating internal links.
  • Content you submit: articles, HTML, and keyword priorities used when running the interlink engine.
  • Generation history: summaries of the inputs and outputs (including linked HTML, priority pairs, link counts, run timestamps) stored for your convenience.

Usage and technical data

We may log IP address, browser user agent, and timestamps for security, rate limiting, and troubleshooting. These logs are kept for a limited period.

Cookies

The Service relies on strictly necessary cookies (e.g., the session cookie issued after login). We do not currently use advertising or tracking cookies.

3. How we use your information

  • To create and maintain your account, authenticate you, and manage secure access.
  • To process your submissions and serve interlinked HTML tailored to your sitemap and priority keywords.
  • To provide history, analytics, and quality of life features (e.g., copy buttons, re-runs) that depend on storing output data.
  • To monitor, debug, and improve platform performance and security.
  • To communicate about service changes, policy updates, or account-specific issues.

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK users)

  • Contractual necessity: Operating the Service, running interlink jobs, and delivering stored history required to fulfill your requests.
  • Legitimate interests: Securing the platform, preventing abuse, improving features, and supporting analytics that do not override your privacy rights.
  • Consent: Optional features, such as receiving marketing communications, will rely on your explicit opt-in (if ever introduced).

5. Data retention

We retain account data while your account remains active and for a reasonable period afterwards to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce agreements. You may request deletion of your account and associated history at any time; we will honor such requests unless we must keep certain data for legal or audit reasons.

6. How we share information

We share data only when necessary:

  • Service providers: Trusted vendors that host our infrastructure, email delivery, or analytics, under data-processing agreements that require confidentiality and security.
  • Legal disclosures: When required by applicable law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, financing, acquisition, or dissolution, your data may be part of the transferred assets. We will provide notice before data is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

We do not sell or rent personal information.

7. International transfers

Your information may be processed in countries other than your own. Where required, we implement safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or rely on adequacy decisions to protect your data during cross-border transfers.

8. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or update the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request deletion or anonymization of your data.
  • Object to or restrict our processing activities.
  • Port your data to another service provider.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below. We will verify your identity before fulfilling your request.
